## Alpha Skill - Hollow Point Shot
<p align="center">
    <img src="https://ultrarumble.com/assets/Character/Ch115/GUI/Skill/T_ui_Skill_Ch115_Unique1.png" width="400" /></br>
</p>

Lady Nagant‚Äôs alpha is pretty basic beaming ability, but it has two main gimmicks that differs it from similar abilities. 

1. The range on her Alpha has an incredibly long range: if you see an enemy, you can hit them. 
2. After landing a hit, it causes a second tick of true damage that ignores shields and deals damage directly to the opponent's HP. This damage can be increased by Tunings, strike cards and other multipliers.
3. Achieving level 4 and level 9 of your alpha makes your shots fly faster.

Other than that, her alpha features higher projectile speed which makes it easier to shoot people at long distances. On the other hand, the size of the projectile is slightly smaller than usual, which is honestly not a big downside.

## Special Action #1 - Scope Mode
<p align="center">
    <img src="https://ultrarumble.com/assets/Character/Ch115/GUI/Skill/T_ui_SpecialSkill_Ch115_1.png" width="400" /></br>
</p>

If you press your special action button, you will enter scoped mode. It has two magnifiers: 3X and 7X. During aiming, you can press your alpha ability button and shoot a different kind of projectile. It doesn‚Äôt scale off your alpha, which means leveling it up doesn‚Äôt make it any stronger. This shot can pierce your opponents, but could be blocked with shield abilities and obstacles. Scoped mode shots do not consume your alpha charges.

<p align="center">
    <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/HydrosPlays/ultrarumbleguide/main/gifs/11500s-gif.gif" width="400" />
</p>

Hitting a head of your enemy will deal extra damage

<p align="center">
    <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/HydrosPlays/ultrarumbleguide/main/gifs/11500s2-gif.gif" width="400" />
</p>

In scoped mode, you can see a laser sight leading to the center of your screen. Keep in mind that this laser sight is visible to your enemies. If you waste too much time aiming, you will warn your enemies of your presence and they will try to evade.

Keep close attention on your laser sight. It shows you a real trajectory of your bullet‚Äôs flight and may warn you that your bullet will hit an obstacle.

<p align="center">
    <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/HydrosPlays/ultrarumbleguide/main/gifs/11500s3-gif.gif" width="400" />
</p>

Jumping or using air walk during Scoped Mode does not cancel it, but using any other quirk skill or roll dodge does. Remember that scoped mode saves your magnifier settings. If you use a 7X magnifier, the next time you enter scoped mode, it will stay on 7X which may be problematic if you need to hit a shot at close range, so you should switch back manually in the meantime.

### Aimed Mode sensitivity
Standard aimed mode sensitivity is not nearly enough for Lady Nagant‚Äôs scoped mode. It feels too slow and unresponsive. Unfortunately, the game does not have dedicated settings for Scoped Mode, but you can slightly increase your aim mode sensitivity to make scoped mode a little more comfortable. Keep in mind that changing this setting will affect all characters, but you can find a middle ground and set it around 120 for PC. 

### Optimizing your gamma reload
Your gamma is your primary mobility skill so make sure you don‚Äôt waste both of your charges unless you really need it. The cooldown only starts off after you touch the ground. Intentionally landing on a high ground or using you melee to land faster helps you reload it.

Another key feature is that your gamma only loses a charge after you make the shot. A start-up animation does not consume you gamma charge which mean you can be knocked out of it and keep the charge, or delay your gamma until the second charge replenishes

### Air Stalling
As a sniper with great mobility and airtime, do not doubt the fact that people down there hate your guts and will jump you the second your feet touch the ground. So it‚Äôs for your better to delay that ‚Äútouching grass‚Äù moment as long as possible. Wisely utilize your airwalk, betas, gammas and gamma delays to stay in the air for maximum amount of time.

### Camping
Your headshots deal devastating damage and you want to hit them as frequently as possible. And what's the easiest target to land a headshot on? Surely a target that does not move. Unfortunately for you, MHUR is a chaotic and fast paced game, so your enemies usually don‚Äôt stand on one spot‚Ä¶ unless they are‚Ä¶ let‚Äôs say opening a box? Saving a civilian? Poor Kota? You can always sit half across the map in front of those points of interest and wait for them to foolishly consider it‚Äôs safe to lock themselves in a long interacting animation so you could capitalize on it.

### Early game aggression
Scoped mode shots do not scale off your alpha quirk level, so it is already at the peak of its power in the very beginning of the game. Landing a headshot in the very beginning of a match will make your underlevelled enemies vulnerable, easy to chase and finish off. Even if they get resurrected by their allies, they would waste too much time of the looting stage which will have a severe consequence on their later gameplay. And hey, also it gives you two free level up cards.

### Hiding your laser sight
Sometimes you want to hold a long angle using your scope, but laser sight makes it too obvious and gives out your position. You can intentionally aim at something closer to you, while keeping said angle in your LoS. This will help you stay unnoticed and catch your enemies off guard with a flickshot.
